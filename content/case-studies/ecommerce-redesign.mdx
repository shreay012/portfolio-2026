## The Brief

A D2C fashion brand had been growing paid acquisition aggressively but was watching most of their ad spend evaporate in the checkout funnel. They were spending $180,000/month on ads and converting at 1.4%. My job: figure out why, and fix it.

The client's hypothesis going in was simple — "our checkout is too long." After a week of analysis, I confirmed they were right, but the problems went much deeper than step count.

---

## Diagnostic Phase

### Analytics Audit

I mapped the full funnel using their Shopify analytics, Google Analytics 4, and Hotjar session recordings:

- **74%** cart abandonment rate (industry avg: 70%)
- Of users who hit the checkout page, **61%** dropped on the shipping information step
- **23%** of drop-offs happened when users were asked to create an account
- Mobile conversion rate: **0.6%** (desktop: 2.8%)

### Session Recording Analysis

I watched 80+ session recordings, looking specifically at mobile sessions in the checkout flow. The patterns were unmistakable:

- Users typed shipping information, only to find their billing address defaulted to a different state
- Promo code fields caused widespread anxiety — users with no code still clicked it, then felt penalized by the expanding form
- The "Create Account" gate before checkout caused an immediate 40%+ drop in the recordings

### Heatmap Analysis

Product Detail Pages (PDPs) showed users scrolling past the size guide before clicking Buy — then returning to size guides after landing on checkout because they'd second-guessed their choice.

---

## Key Problems Identified

1. **Account creation forced before checkout** — Industry research (and our recordings) confirms: forced registration at checkout is the #1 killer of conversion
2. **7-step checkout with excessive fields** — Requiring delivery instructions, phone number, and account creation on separate steps
3. **Promo code field anxiety** — Expanding form triggered users without codes to search for codes, abandoning cart
4. **PDP to checkout context loss** — Key decision-making information (size guide, materials, return policy) disappeared at checkout
5. **Mobile layout failures** — CTAs buried below the fold, form inputs without autofill optimization, no Apple Pay / Google Pay surface

---

## Design Solutions

### Guest Checkout + Smart Account (2 Steps)

I restructured checkout entirely:

**Step 1**: Email + Express pay options (Apple Pay, Google Pay, Shop Pay) — reducing checkout to a single tap for ~35% of mobile users.

**Step 2**: Shipping + Payment on one screen for users not using express checkout. Auto-fill enabled, smart defaults, same-as-shipping billing defaulted to ON.

Account creation moved to **post-purchase** — "Save your details for next time?" — where it converted at 41% with no drop-off cost.

### Contextual PDP Design

I redesigned Product Detail Pages to surface the three most common pre-checkout questions inline: size guide (collapsible), material composition, and return policy — all within the buy zone.

Added a sticky buy bar on mobile that revealed when the primary CTA scrolled off-screen, keeping the purchase action always accessible.

### Promo Code Field

Moved promo code to an unobtrusive link below the order summary: "Have a promo code?" This eliminated the anxiety trigger for users without codes and maintained visibility for those with codes.

### Trust Signal Layer

Added three trust signals to the checkout header: secure payment badge, free returns reminder, and delivery estimation. Users who saw these signals on mobile had a 22% higher checkout completion rate.

### Mobile-First Rebuild

Complete mobile checkout rebuild with:
- Touch-optimized input sizing (minimum 44px targets)
- Keyboard type optimization (`type="email"`, `type="tel"`, `autocomplete` attributes)
- Apple Pay and Google Pay as primary CTA on mobile
- Reduced form to essential fields only (removed phone, removed delivery notes from required)

## Outcomes

| Overall conversion rate | 1.4% | 2.03% | **+45%** |
| Cart abandonment | 74% | 49% | **−34%** |

| Checkout-to-purchase | 39% | 61% | **+56%** |

## ROI Context

At their $180k/month ad spend and previous conversion rate of 1.4%, they were generating roughly 2,520 orders/month. At the new 2.03% rate, that's ~3,654 — **1,134 additional orders per month** from the same ad spend, with zero additional acquisition cost.

---

## Lessons

E-commerce optimization is almost always a trust problem masquerading as a UX problem. The checkout steps weren't the root cause — they were symptoms. The real issue was that at every decision point, the design left users with unresolved anxiety: *"Is this the right size? Can I return it? Is this site secure?"*

**Remove the anxiety. Remove the obstacle. People buy.**
